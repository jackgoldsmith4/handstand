# 100-Day Handstand Plan

**Goal:** Achieve a 3–10 second freestanding handstand by Day 100.
**Equipment:** None. Bodyweight only. Just a wall and some floor space.
**Daily time:** 20–30 minutes, 5–6 days/week.
**Rest days:** 1–2 per week. Active recovery (walk, light stretch) is fine.

---

## Before You Start: Self-Assessment

Before Day 1, check these prerequisites:
- [ ] You can bend your wrists to 90° without pain
- [ ] You can raise both arms fully overhead without arching your lower back
- [ ] You can hold a 30-second plank
- [ ] You can do 5 push-ups

If you can't do these yet, spend 1–2 weeks on just the wrist warm-up, hollow body hold, and shoulder mobility before starting Day 1. Most people can start Day 1 immediately.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Short daily sessions beat long infrequent ones.** The handstand is a neurological skill — your brain needs daily rehearsal to wire the balance reflexes.
2. **Never skip the wrist warm-up.** Wrists adapt more slowly than muscle. Injuring them will derail everything.
3. **Chest to wall, not back to wall.** Back-to-wall trains a banana arch. Always face the wall.
4. **Stop on a good rep, not failure.** Fatigued technique builds bad motor patterns.
5. **Fear of falling is the #1 blocker.** You'll learn to bail safely in Week 1. This changes everything.

## The Mandatory Wrist Warm-Up (5 min — do this EVERY session)

| Exercise | Reps/Hold |
|----------|-----------|
| Wrist circles (all fours, palms out — both directions) | 10 each direction |
| Kneeling wrist extension rocks (lean shoulders past wrists) | 10 reps + 10-sec hold |
| Prayer stretch (palms together, push downward) | 30 sec |
| Reverse prayer stretch (backs of hands together, push downward) | 30 sec |
| Quadruped forward rocks (rock from heel-of-hand to knuckles) | 10 forward + 10 side-to-side |
| Fingers-back stretch (all fours, fingers point toward knees — lean back) | 10 reps + 10-sec hold |
| Wrist flexion (tops of hands on floor, lean backward) | 10 reps + 10-sec hold |
| Knuckle roll-offs (fist on floor → open wide → roll onto fingertips) | 10 reps |
| Fingertip push-ups | 3 × 5 |

# PHASE 3: Freestanding Development (Days 68–100)

**Goal:** Achieve a 3–10 second straight-body freestanding handstand.

**Structure of every Phase 3 session:**
1. Wrist warm-up (5 min)
2. Pirouette bail: 10 bails
3. Conditioning: 2 × chest-to-wall with shoulder taps
4. Freestanding practice: 15–20 minutes (kick-ups, wall floats, tuck holds, straddle, max holds)
5. Conditioning: hollow body, pike push-ups
6. Flexibility (3x per week)

# Quick Reference: Key Exercises

## Hollow Body Hold (The Foundation of Every Handstand)
Lie on your back. Press your lower back completely flat into the floor — no gap. Lift your legs and shoulders off the ground. Arms extended overhead. Think: a hollow banana shape. This full-body tension is what you maintain in a handstand. Start with knees tucked, progress to legs straight.

## Chest-to-Wall Handstand (Your Main Drill for Weeks 3–10)
Face the wall. Place hands 15–20 cm from wall. Kick up and place your CHEST (not back) against the wall. Lock elbows. Tuck pelvis (flatten lower back). Push through your shoulders — don't let them collapse. This position is your goal handstand shape.

## Fingertip Pressure Drill (The Core Balance Mechanism)
In your chest-to-wall handstand: deliberately shift your weight forward until you feel significant pressure on your fingertips (not your palm heel). Hold 3 seconds. Shift back. This is how you prevent falling forward in a freestanding handstand — it's a trainable reflex.

## Pirouette Bail (Learn Before You Fear It)
Kick up to handstand against wall. Intentionally overbalance forward (lean past vertical). Rotate one hand 90° outward, step that arm around, and cartwheel to standing. Practice this until it's automatic. Fear of falling will otherwise cap how hard you kick.

## Kick-Up Technique
Lunge stance. Dominant leg forward (it stays down longer, controlling how far you go). Place hands shoulder-width, directly below your shoulders. Kick back leg up with CONTROL — not aggression. Let front leg follow. The goal is to barely reach vertical. Overshooting is the most common mistake.

# Troubleshooting

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Wrist pain | More warm-up. Back off volume. Add fingertip push-ups. |
| Banana arch in handstand | More hollow body holds. Focus on tucking pelvis at the wall. |
| Kicking too far every time | Kick softer. Use a smaller step in your lunge. |
| Can't kick far enough | Stretch hip flexors. Lunge deeper. |
| Shaky/collapsing shoulders | More scapular push-ups, shoulder shrugs in plank. |
| Falling forward constantly | Fingertip pressure drill. Practice this daily. |
| Fear of kicking up | 10 pirouette bails at the start of every session. |
| Progress stalled | Are you doing the session 5–6x/week? Daily frequency matters more than anything. |
| Arms bending | Reduce difficulty. More pike push-ups, more wall hold time. |

# Beyond Day 100

If you've hit consistent 5+ second holds, here's where to go next:
- **Hold time:** Work toward 30-second, then 60-second freestanding holds
- **Shape:** Perfect the straight-line: ears between arms, hollow core, pointed toes
- **Entry:** Develop a consistent one-legged kick-up with no wall
- **Variations:** Straddle handstand, tuck handstand, handstand push-ups (pike → wall → freestanding)
- **Press handstand:** The ultimate skill. Requires significant compression and shoulder strength.
